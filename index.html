About Me

A year ago I was knee-deep in graduate school applications when I had to ask myself: will this path bring me the lifelong career fulfillment that I desire? Although it was rewarding to bring an impact into patients' lives, there is typically one best way to treat a condition and deviating from the textbook could be dangerous. I found myself yearning for an environment powered by critical thinking and creativity and thought about how one of my favorite parts of being a medical assistant was using the Electronic Medical Records. The software we used was wildly outdated and encountered frequent glitches and I wanted to determine the causes behind those bugs and remodel the system to improve its usability. One bootcamp and countless hours of coding later, I now wake up with the same feeling of excitement and fulfillment that I had been looking for a year ago and hope to still bring an impact into people's lives, just in a slightly different way.
